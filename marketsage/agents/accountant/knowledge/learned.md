# Learnings


- [2026-04-27] Market sentiment analysis for mega-cap equities is increasingly incorporating data from prediction markets (e.g., Polymarket) to gauge earnings 'beat' probabilities, supplementing traditional retail and institutional sentiment.

- [2026-04-27] Gold is transitioning from a speculative safe-haven to a 'neutral reserve asset' as institutional trust in US Treasuries erodes due to high sovereign debt levels.

- [2026-04-27] A 'funding premium' is being applied to long-term projects as the reliability of US Treasuries as a stable 'risk-free rate' provider diminishes.

- [2026-04-27] The 'Risk-Off disguised as Risk-On' phenomenon occurs when equity indices remain stable because investors use them as a hedge against currency debasement rather than a bet on economic growth.

- [2026-04-29] When analyzing REITs, traditional P/E ratios are often inflated and misleading due to heavy non-cash depreciation charges lowering GAAP Net Income. FFO (Funds From Operations) and AFFO (Adjusted FFO) are the standard performance metrics. Additionally, REIT valuations are highly sensitive to the 10-Year Treasury yield; tracking the 'yield spread' (REIT Dividend Yield minus 10-Year Yield) is essential for assessing relative value.
