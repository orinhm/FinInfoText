# Learnings


- [2026-04-28] Ticker confusion exists for 'GOLD': In some financial data sources, it refers to Gold.com, Inc. (formerly A-Mark Precious Metals, a bullion trading firm), while it traditionally represents the primary miner Barrick Gold Corporation on the NYSE.

- [2026-04-28] Barrick Gold's Reko Diq copper-gold project in Pakistan faces significant capex blowouts above its original $5.6B-$6.0B Phase 1 estimate and has been postponed until mid-2027 due to supply chain costs and geopolitical conflicts.

- [2026-04-28] Barrick Gold is executing a strategic M&A pivot away from high-risk African and Asian jurisdictions, actively targeting North American acquisitions (particularly junior explorers in Nevada near existing operations) to replenish reserves.

- [2026-04-28] The ticker symbol GOLD corresponds to Gold.com, Inc. (formerly A-Mark Precious Metals, rebranded in Dec 2025), a precious metals retail/wholesale platform, and does not represent the miner Barrick Gold in this context.

- [2026-04-28] Gold.com (GOLD) thrives on high market volatility and retail panic; its physical bullion dealing business model profits heavily from high transaction volumes regardless of the directional price of gold, though it operates on razor-thin margins.
