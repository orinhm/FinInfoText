# Learnings


- [2026-04-26] A significant jump in AISC (e.g., from US$900 to >US$2,100/oz) between successive PEAs by different owners typically indicates a correction of historical over-optimism regarding vein widths and mining dilution.

- [2026-04-26] In high-grade orogenic systems (e.g., Fosterville-style), intercepts exceeding 1,000 gram-metres are statistical outliers that necessitate aggressive top-cutting in Mineral Resource Estimates (MRE) to ensure resource integrity.

- [2026-04-26] Acquiring existing brownfield infrastructure (like the Pine Cove Mill) is a strategic shortcut that can bypass 3-5 years of permitting lead times for new mining projects.

- [2026-04-26] A high-cost 'starter mine' can function as a strategic 'bridge to production,' utilizing debt and cash flow to fund a larger flagship development and neutralize the 'Orphan Period' dilution risk on the Lassonde Curve.

- [2026-04-27] The 'Orphan Period' of the Lassonde Curve (transition from exploration to development) can trigger significant equity corrections (~40%) even in fully funded junior miners as speculative discovery buyers exit before institutional production buyers enter.

- [2026-04-27] Strategic acquisition of brownfield infrastructure (e.g., existing mills) is a critical derisking event that can bypass 3-5 years of permitting, effectively compressing the 'Orphan Period' for junior miners.

- [2026-04-27] Junior developers trading at a high Price to Book (P/B) ratio (e.g., >2.0) compared to peer averages (0.5x–1.2x P/NAV) are highly sensitive to 'Integrity Gates' such as AISC projections; exceeding $1,800/oz can trigger a re-rating toward book value.

- [2026-04-27] A high Current Ratio (e.g., >3.5) in exploration-stage miners provides a technical 'solvency floor' but does not necessarily protect the share price from sector-wide volatility or a high Beta (e.g., >1.90).
