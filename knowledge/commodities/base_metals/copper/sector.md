# Learnings


- [2026-04-28] Gold-copper explorer Meridian is planning a London listing to access deeper, more sophisticated institutional capital pools.

- [2026-04-28] Meridian (gold-copper) is actively seeking a dual listing in London to tap into institutional liquidity and fund supply expansion.

- [2026-04-28] Capital in the Latin American copper mining sector is actively engaging in jurisdictional arbitrage, shifting away from Chile due to regulatory sluggishness and towards Argentina's emerging, capital-friendly environment.

- [2026-04-28] Extreme copper supply constraints, driven by sovereign interventions and aging mine profiles, are placing a 'scarcity premium' on de-risked, near-production assets in stable tier-one jurisdictions (e.g., North America, Australia).

- [2026-04-28] Due to terrestrial permitting constraints and a structural supply deficit, speculative capital in the copper industry is increasingly funding frontier technologies, such as deep-sea polymetallic nodule extraction.

- [2026-04-28] The competitive landscape for copper mining in South America is shifting, with Argentina increasingly attracting capital and inching ahead of traditionally dominant but recently sluggish Chile.

- [2026-04-28] The closure of First Quantum's Cobre Panamá mine has created a structural supply shock, driving a widening valuation premium for copper producers operating in safe, Tier-1 jurisdictions over those in volatile regions.

- [2026-04-29] Mega-cap copper producers can temporarily decouple from record-high commodity spot prices ($12,500+/MT) due to severe idiosyncratic operational risks (e.g., major mine output cuts or restarts like FCX's Grasberg) or sudden leadership vacuums (e.g., unexpected CEO death at SCCO), leading to 15-20% multiple contractions despite high underlying free cash flow potential.
