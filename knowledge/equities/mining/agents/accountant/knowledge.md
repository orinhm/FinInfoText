# Learnings


- [2026-04-27] In the junior mining 'Lassonde Curve,' the transition from explorer to developer often triggers an 'Orphan Period' where stock price diverges negatively from spot gold prices due to capital-intensive construction and investor rotation.

- [2026-04-27] A P/Book ratio above 2.0 for a junior developer (relative to a peer average of 0.5x-1.2x) typically signals market pricing of strategic infrastructure, such as a permitted mill or district consolidation, rather than just mineral resource value.

- [2026-04-27] A 'heavy' share structure (e.g., >300M shares) in a junior developer resulting from district consolidation increases sensitivity to further equity dilution, making grade performance more critical than commodity price fluctuations for NPV stability.

- [2026-04-27] Gold has undergone a structural 'Great Decoupling' where it no longer consistently correlates inversely with real rates or the USD, driven by aggressive central bank accumulation (the 'China Floor').

- [2026-04-27] The 'Mining Sector Paradox' describes a regime where producers generate record free cash flow and margins (up to $3,000/oz) yet trade at significant valuation discounts (P/NAV 0.2x-0.5x) relative to the underlying metal price.

- [2026-04-27] Major gold producers are prioritizing 'safe jurisdiction' M&A (e.g., Finland consolidation) and capital discipline (dividends/buybacks) over aggressive production expansion even during record price peaks.

- [2026-04-27] The 'Great Decoupling' refers to a macro-financial shift where gold and energy prices rise in tandem, breaking the historical inverse correlation between high oil prices and gold, signaling an inflationary regime favoring hard assets over sovereign debt.

- [2026-04-27] The 'Mining Sector Paradox' describes a market condition where junior developers trade at severe P/NAV discounts (e.g., 0.35x) despite record commodity spot prices, driven by institutional capital favoring dividend-paying producers over explorers.

- [2026-04-27] In a high-energy price environment ($90+/bbl), energy costs account for approximately 20-25% of a mine's direct operating costs, causing margin compression for producers with AISC in the upper quartiles.

- [2026-04-28] Bullion trading and precious metals financial services firms (like Gold.com/A-Mark) operate on exceptionally high revenue but razor-thin net margins (e.g., ~0.08%), with profitability driven by transaction volumes, bid-ask spreads, and lending, rather than direct exposure to gold spot price appreciation.

## AISC Benchmarks (Gold, USD/oz, 2025-2026)
| Quartile | AISC Range | Assessment |
|----------|-----------|-----------|
| Q1 (lowest cost) | < $1,200 | Excellent — strong margins at any gold price |
| Q2 | $1,200 – $1,500 | Good — profitable above $2,000 gold |
| Q3 | $1,500 – $1,900 | Average — needs strong gold price |
| Q4 (highest cost) | > $1,900 | High cost — risk at gold downturns |

Note: Development-stage AISC includes first-fill costs and will normalize lower in steady-state.

## NPV / IRR Thresholds
- NPV > $500M at 5% discount → major-scale project
- NPV > $100M at 5% discount → viable development project
- IRR > 30% → excellent, will attract financing easily
- IRR > 15% → viable, standard threshold for go/no-go
- IRR < 15% → marginal, needs higher commodity price or cost reduction
